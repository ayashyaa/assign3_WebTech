My Story

My name is Leila, I am 31 years old, and a mother of two children — a son and a daughter (12 and 10). I am a designer and the founder of my own studio.

I had very few childhood photos (I was the third child in the family, and times were tough). But my path to design began in childhood — I loved drawing, rearranging furniture, and helping with repairs. I even mixed flour paste to glue wallpaper myself, which lasted almost 10 years.

I never missed an episode of “School of Renovation” or “Housing Question” on TV. At 11, I already knew I would become a designer, even though in Kazakhstan the profession was not popular.

At 18, I entered the Academy of Architecture. People tried to discourage me, saying there was no demand. I started as a sales manager in a company supplying design projects. That gave me confidence and knowledge of the design and architecture market.

Later, I worked in a construction company, learning the technical side of building. Then I joined a design studio in St. Petersburg, where I gained valuable experience. After six months, I realized I couldn’t work for someone else — I quit and took my first independent projects.

Word of mouth began to spread. It was scary, but as they say, there is no self‑development without risk. Now I work in a studio with a stunning view of the city and mountains, implementing amazing projects. Today, La Home Studio operates in Europe — something I never even dreamed of.
